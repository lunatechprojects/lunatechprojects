Water

The water cycle

The water of great oceans, rivers, seas and lakes evaporates into the atmosphere as water vapour. This water varpour condenses on reaching the cooler part of the atmosphere to form clouds and eventually falls to the earth again as rain. A lot of rain water goes into the rivers, seas and oceans as surface water, while the remainder sinks under the ground. The undergroud water eventually finds its way into the rivers which in turn flows into the seas and oceans, from where it is again evaporated in the atmosphere. This circulation of water is known as the water cycle.

Hardness of water

In some areas, soap will not easily form a lather with water. When this happens, the water is said to be hard. Hardness happens when rain water trickling across rocks and through ground and dissolving various amounts of chemical compounds. As calcium and magnesium ions are the main cause of hardness in water. there are two types of hardness, temporary and permanent hardness.

Temporary Hardness

This occurs when calcium and/ or magnesium hydrogen carbonates are dissolved in water. This happens when rain water which has dissolved some carbon dioxide fronm the atmosphere forming a weak acidic solution, falls on rocks containing calcium and magnesium carbonates.
CaCO3(s) + H2O(l) +CO2(g) → Ca(HCO3)2(aq)
Temporary hardness can be removed by boiling. The boiling decomposes the hydrogen carbonate and thus removes the source of hardness.
Ca(HCO3)2(aq) → CaCO3(s) + H2O(l) + CO2(g)

Permanent Hardness

Permant hardness is caused by calcium and/or magnesium suphates in water. These compuonds occur naturally and, although calcium sulphate is only slightly soluble it dissolves sufficiently to cause hardness. Permanent hardness cannot be removed by boiling. It can be softened by:
1) Adding washing soda, (sodium carbonate) to the water.
CaSO4(aq) + Na2CO3(aq) → CaCO3(s) + Na2SO4(aq) This removes the calcium ions from the solution.
2) Distillation
3) Ion exchange. This method is used for softening water on a large scale.Certain complex sodium compounds remove dissolved ions from hard water and replace them with their own.
CaSO4 + Na complex → Ca complex + Na2SO4

Action of Hard water with soap

One of our commonest substances is sodium stearate (C17H35COONa). The action of soap with water is its ability to dissolve readily in water. Solution soap is able to dislodge the particles of dirt. With hard water, which contains calcium and magnesium salts, soap cannot readily dissolve and, theefore it is not able to act as a cleansing agent eeasily. In hard water soap reacts with calcium sulphate to form insoluble calcium stearate which appears as scum.
CaSO4(aq) + 2Nast(s) → Na2SO4(aq) + Cast2(S) (Calcium stearate)
Only when the calcium sulphate has been removed can the soap form a permanent lather.

Detergents and their use

Synthentic soaps have been discovered. These can produce a permanent lather and clean substances in both hard and soft waters. Their reaction does not produce any insoluble scum to retard the cleansing action aand the calcium/magnesium compuonds they form are soluble in water.These soaps are called 'detergents'.The are sold on market in varoius names
e.g Fab, Omo, Teepol, Magic, Nomi and Frolic
