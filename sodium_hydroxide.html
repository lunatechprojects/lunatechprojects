Industrial Preparation of Sodium Hydroxide

Diaphragm cell method

The cell consists of saturated sodium chloride solution in a porous diaphragm which is supported on outside by a perforated steel cathode.
The anode is a graphite rod suspended in brine electrolyte. Ions present in the electolytic solution are Na+, Cl- from sodium chloride and H+, OH- from water.
At the cathode
H+ and Na+ ions collect through the diaghragm and the H+ ions are preferentially dicharged as hydrogen gas.
2H+ + 2e- → H2(g)


	At the anode

Cl- and OH- ions collect and the Cl- ions are preferentially discharged as chlorine gas.

	2Cl- -  2e- → Cl2(g)

Thus a strong solution of sodium hydroxide is produced. Steam is blown into the cell during this process and this steam condenses 
diluting the sodium hydroxide solution which diffuses through the diaphragm cell and collects at the base of the cell
The arrangement of the cell is such that the liberated chlorine gas is removed to prevent it from reacting with hydroxide ions 
to form sodium chlorate.

	
Uses of sodium Hydroxide
 
1. Used in the manufacture of soap

2.Neutralisation of acids to form mineral salts

3.Preparation of insoluble metallic hydroxides.

4.Manufacture of man made fabric.

5.In the extraction of aluminium from its ores.

6.Used in paper industry.
