Industrial Preparation of Oxygen

Because large amounts of oxygen are used in the industry. It has to be produced in large quantities. The most economical source of oxygen is the air and both oxygen and notogen can be obtained by fractional distillation of liquid air.


1)Dust particles are first removed from the air and the carbon dioxide and water are removed because at low temperatures they would solididfy and block the apparatus.
2)The air is compressed to a pressure of about a pressure of 200atms and passed through a water-cooled coil to cool it back to its original temperature.
3)The cool, compressed air is then allowed to expand by passing it through a jet into an expansion chamber.This expansion causes it to cool further.
4)The coooled air circulates around the apparatus until the temperature is low enough for it to be liquidised.
5)The components of liqid air aremaily liquid nitrogen( b.p -196oC) and liuid oxygen (b.p -183oC) Which are seperated by fractional distillation.Liquid nitrogen boils first leaving the liquid oxygen behind.
6)The liquid oxygen can be stored as a liquid or converted to a gas and stored in steel cylinders at pressures of 100 atms.

Uses of oxygen

1. Oxygen is necessary for respiration and therefore for life itself. During respiration oxygen is taken into the body and is used to xidises food materials producing energy for body activities.
There is usually sufficient oxygen in air for respiration to take place but sometimes in hospitals pure oxygen is used to improve respiration in patients from lung diseases.Peolple such as mountaineers, astronauts and underwater divers carry oxygen cylinders so that they ae able to breathe even though air is not available
2. Oxygen is essential for burning and enables vital combustion processes to take place.
3. Liquid oxygen is used in rockets.
4. A mixture of oxygen and acetylene is burnt to form oxyacetylene flame which is used to weld metals
