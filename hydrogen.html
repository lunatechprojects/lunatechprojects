Industrial Preparation of Hydrogen

Hydrogen is obtained from either either natural gas or a mixture of hydrocarbons containing 5-9 carbon atoms known as naphtha
i) catalysis with steam at high pressure and temperature
C7H16(g) + H2O(g) → 7CO(g) + 15H2(g)
(Natural gas) CH4(g) +H2O(g) - CO(g) +3H2(g)
ii)non-catalytic partial oxidation witth oxygen at high pressure
C7H16(g) + O2(g) → 14CO(g) + 16H2(g)
(Natural gas) CH4(g) +O2(g) &raarr; 2CO(g) +4H2(g)

Uses of Hydrogen

1.It is extensively used in large scale manufacture of ammonia and in synthensis of hydrogen chloride and methanol
2.Water gas, a mixture of carbon monoxide and hydrogen is used as fuel.
3.Used as a reucing agent, it is used to reduce metallic oxides to their metals.
4.Used in the conversion of coal to synthetic 'petrol'.
5.Used in the hardening of oils to produce margarine.
6.The oxy-hydrogen blowpipe and atomic hydrogen torch are used to produce high temperatures for welding and cutting metals.
7.Sometimes used for filling light bulbs and because of its lightness, balloons although care has to be taken because of its inflammability.
