Pepration of Methanol

Methanol is obtained industrially from methane. A mixture of methane obtained from natural gas and steam is passed over a nickel ctatalyst at temperatures of 900oC. The product, a mixture of carbon monoxide and hydrogen is passed over amixture of metallic oxides catalysts (zinc and chromium (III) oxide) at temperature between 300 and 400oC and a pressure of 200 atm. Methanol is produced.
CH4(g) + H2O(g) → CO(g) + 3H2(g) ( Ni catalyst)
CO(G) + H2(g) → CH3OH(g) (zinc and chromium (III) oxide)

Uses of methanol

1. Used as solvent for paints and varnishes.
2. Used as a denaturing agent for ethanol.
3. Used in the manufacture of methanal (formaldehyde) and other organic compounds.
