Industrial preparation of Sodium carbonate

The Solvay Process

Sodium carbonate is manufactured by the solvay process
Basically carbon reacts with ammoniacal brine in the solvay tower.
Limestone is heated in a lime kiln where it decomposes into calcium oxide and carbon dioxide
CaCO3(s) → CaO(s) + CO2(g)
Ammoniacal brine is obtained by passing ammonia gas into a tank containing concetrated solution of brine. This ammoniacal brine is then passed into the solvay tower at the top while carbon dioxide enters at the bottom of the tower. The brine passes down the tower and is exposed on the metal baffles for affective contact and reaction with carbon dioxide The sodium hydrogen carbonate produced is not in such concetrated solution as other products and it collects at the base of the tower. Soluble ammonium chloride is also produced.
NaCl(aq) + H2O(l) + NH3(g) + CO2(g) → NaHCO3(s) + NH4CL(aq)
The insoluble sodium hydrogen carbonate is filtered, washed and dried and then heated to produce white anyhydrous sodium carbonate
NaHCO3(S) → Na2CO3(s) + H2O(g) + CO2(g)
The carbon dioxide gas is recycled in th process and hydrated sodium carbonate is obtained by crystallising the anyhydrous salt in hot water.
Na2CO3(s) + 10H2O(l) → Na2CO3,10H2O(s)

Uses of sodium carbonate

1.Used to soften hard water
2.Manufacture of glass
3.Precipitate metallic carbonates in qualitative analysis
4.Manufacture of detergents.
