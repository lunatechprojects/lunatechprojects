Industrial Preparation of Nitrogen

Because large amounts of nitrogen are used in the industry. It has to be produced in large quantities. The most economical source of nirogen is the air and both oxygen and nitrogen can be obtained by fractional distillation of liquid air.
1)Dust particles are first removed from the air and the carbon dioxide and water are removed because at low temperatures they would solididfy and block the apparatus.
2)The air is compressed to a pressure of about 200atms and passed through a water-cooled coil to cool it back to its original temperature.
3)The cool, compressed air is then allowed to expand by passing it through a jet into an expansion chamber.This expansion causes it to cool further.
4)The coooled air circulates around the apparatus until the temperature is low enough for it to be liquidised.
5)The components of liqid air are mainly liquid nitrogen( b.p -196oC) and liquid oxygen (b.p -183oC) Which are seperated by fractional distillation.Liquid nitrogen boils
first leaving the liquid oxygen behind.
6)It it is collected and stored in containers.

Uses of Nitrogen
