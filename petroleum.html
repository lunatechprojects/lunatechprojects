Fractional Distillation of petroleum

As petroluem is a complex mixture of gases, liquids and solids.It must b reined into componenst. This is done by fractional distillatin in an oil refinery.
The crude oil is first heated in seperate furnace to temperature of 400oC. The hot liquid is the passed into a fractionating tower. The tower consists of trays and bubllecaps placed at different levels or temperatures. Each tray is cooler than the one below it as it is little further from the source of heat. As the hot mixture of gases and vapours passes up the tower, the various vapours condense at different levels according to their boiling points. The products which are gases at room' temperatures leave at the top of the tower. The fractions collected in each tray are tapped off and redistilled.

Main products of petroleum and physical states

1.hydrogen. methane, ethane, butane (gases, b.p= 40oC)
2. Petroleum ether (liquid, b.p=100oC)
3. Petrol (liqiud, b.p=200oC)
4. Kerosene/paraffin (liquid, b.p=200oC)
5. Diesel (liquid, b.p= 250-350oC)
6. paraffin wax, petroleum jelly, vaseline (solids, b.p= 400oC)
7. asphalt or bitumen (solids, b.p= 400oC)

Uses of petroleum fractions

1.Gases in gaseous fractions are used for fuels.They are sold as gases or liquids under high pressure
2.Low boiling point liquids are used as solvents
3.The naphtha or gasoline fractions produce petrol used in motor engines.Petrol is used as solvent in paints.
4.Kerosene is used in lamps and as a major fuel in jet engines.
5. Diesel is used in heavy duty engines such as lorries, trains and tractors
6. Asphat or bitumen is uesd to suraface roads
7.Heavy oils are used to heat furnaces and boilers
8.Grease as used as lubricants in movable parts of machines.
