Industrial Preparation of Chlorine

Using a Mercury Cathode Cell

At the Cathode;
Sodium is discharged preferentially to hydrogen and dissolves in the mercury to form a sodium amalgam (Na/Hg) Sodium Amalgam is passed through a trough below the cell which contains water. Here it reacts to form a solution of sodium hydroxide and hyrogen is liberated.The mercury is regenerated and used again.
2Na/Hg(l) + 2H2O(g) → 2NaOH(aq) +2Hg(l) + H2(g)
At the anode;
The chloride ions from the brine are discharged as chlorine gas is led away.
2CL- - 2e- → Cl2(g)

Uses of Chlorine

1. Ued in the manufacture of inseticides, antiseptics and weed killers.
2. Used for bleaching purposes such as textiles.
3. Sterilising water for both domestic and industrial use.
4. Manufacture of Hydrchloric acid, tetrachloromethane and and chloral
5. Manufacture of Plastics e.g (P.V.C)
6. Treatment of sewage.
