Industrial Preparation of Sulphuric acid

The Contact process

Sulphur is burnt in the presence of air to release sulphur dioxide
S(s) + O2(g) → SO2(g)
The gas evolved (sulphur dioxide) is cleaned and dried.It is then passed over catalyst where it is heated in the presence of air over a vanadium (v) oxide catalyst to a temperature of 450'C. The sulphur dioxide reacts with oxygen in air to produce sulphur trioxide.
SO2(g) + O2(g) = SO2(g) (reversible reaction)
The sulphur trioxide gas is then passed into an absorption chamber where is is dissolved in concetrated ssulphuric acid to produce oleum (fuming sulphuric acid)
SO3(g) + H2SO4(l) → H2S2O7(l)
The oleum is then diluted with water to produce dilute sulphuric acid
H2S2O7(l) + H2O(l) → H2SO4(aq)

Uses of sulphuric acid

1. Used in the manufature of fertilisers
2. Used in the extraction of metals and pickling metal surfaces
3. In the manufacture of synthentic fibres
4. In the manufacture of paints, explosives and dyes
5. Used in the manufacture of other chemiclas like hydrochloric acid, sulphates, alums and nitric acid
6. Used as an electrolyte in batteries
7. Used in the refining of petroleum.
