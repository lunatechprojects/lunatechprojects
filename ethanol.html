Preparation of Ethanol

Ethanol can be manufactured through a reaction between ethene and sulphuric acid to form ethyl hydrogen sulphate and subsequent hydration
C2H4(g) +H2SO4(aq) → C2H5HSO4(l)
 C2H5HSO4(l) + H2O(l) → C2H5OH(l) + H2SO4(l)
But it can be manufactured from also complex organic compounds like sugars, cellulose and alcohols. The breakdown is by certain microbes present in plant and animal cells.

Alternative methods


(1)Ethanol from Starches


The maize corn or cassava is crushed and heated in steam under pressure to extract the starch. The enzyme diastase is used to catalyse the decomosition of the starch into sugar, Diastase is present in maltwhich itself is present in partially germinated barley. Malt is added to the starch extract and the mixture is kept at a temperature of about 60oC for about an hour. The product is maltose sugar and the equation for the reaction is as follows.
2(C6H10O5)n(s) + H2O(l) → C12H22O11(aq) (diastase)
This is the first stage in fermentation process
The next involves the fermentation of maltose to alcohol. Yeast a fungus plant, is added to the maltose. Yaest contains a number of enzymes, including maltase and zymase. The maltase catalyses the conversion of maltose to glucose a simpler sugar.
C12H22O11(aq) + H2O(l) → C6H12O6(aq) (maltase)
The other enzyme, zymase then catalyses the fermenting of glucose into ethanol and carbon dioxide.
C12H22O11(aq) → C2H5OH(aq) +2CO2(g)
The resulting solution is 11-14% alcohol as yeast is killed.By solutions of more than this amount. In industry, this solution is fractionally distilled to obtain rectified spirit containing 95% ethanol and 5% water

(2)Ethanol from cellulose


Cellulose fibres, from cotton lint for example, are steeped in dilute acid and heated under pressure. This converts the fibres into glucose sugar. The excess acid is neutralised and the glucose solution seperated. The yeast is addedto glucose and zymase in the yeast converts the glucose to ethanol
(C6H10O5)n(s) + nH2O(l) → nC6H12O6(aq)
C6H12O6(aq) → 2C2H5OH(aq) + 2CO2(g)

Uses of ethanol

1. Used as solvent in paints and vanishes
2. In the manufacture of ethyl ethanoate (ethyl acetate) and ethanoic acid
3. Used as preservative and sterilising liquid
4. Used in alcoholic drinks e.g beers, wines and spirits.
