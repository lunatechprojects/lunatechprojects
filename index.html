Preface

The changes taking place in the chemistry course throughout the world today should not not be measured only in terms of content, but also in terms of teaching methods used.
The aim of teaching science and (mathematics) is to achieve 'process' goals as well as 'product' goals.
One important reason for this change in emphasis is that countries are becoming increasingly more aware of their obligations towards pupils for whom O'level will mark their end of formal education
In the mordern world of nuclear energy and micro-computers, the study of chemistry provides pupils with thought-patterns which are considered essential in aquiring such a balanced view.
